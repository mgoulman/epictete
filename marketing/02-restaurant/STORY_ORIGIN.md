## The Name: Epictetus (Épictète)

### Who Was Epictetus?
Epictetus (c. 50-135 AD) was a Greek Stoic philosopher born into slavery in Hierapolis, Phrygia (modern-day Turkey). Despite his humble origins, he became one of the most influential Stoic teachers in history.

### Key Philosophical Teachings

**1. The Dichotomy of Control**
> "Some things are within our power, while others are not."

Epictetus taught that we should focus our energy only on what we can control — our thoughts, actions, and choices — while accepting what we cannot control with grace.

**2. Mindful Living**
> "It is not things that disturb us, but our judgments about things."

He emphasized the importance of being present, making conscious choices, and finding contentment through wisdom rather than external circumstances.

**3. The Art of Mastery**
> "No man is free who is not master of himself."

Self-discipline and intentional action were central to his philosophy.

## The Connection to Dining

### "Savoure chaque instant, maîtrise chaque choix"
*(Savor every moment, master every choice)*

This tagline beautifully bridges Stoic philosophy with the dining experience:

| Stoic Principle | Dining Translation |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Be Present** | Savor each bite, engage all senses |
| **Make Intentional Choices** | Choose quality over quantity |
| **Find Joy in Simplicity** | Appreciate honest, well-prepared food |
| **Master the Self** | Mindful eating, conscious consumption |
| **Accept What You Cannot Control** | Trust the chef, embrace the experience |

1. **Mediterranean Heritage** — Stoic philosophy originated in the Mediterranean world, connecting to the cuisine
2. **Farm-to-Table Philosophy** — Knowing where food comes from is intentional living
3. **Slow Food Movement** — Counter to fast-food culture, embracing patience
4. **Quality Focus** — Choosing the best ingredients reflects wise choices
5. **Present-Moment Dining** — Creating experiences that encourage guests to slow down

## The Origin Story

### The Vision
*(Note: This section should be verified/expanded with actual founder information)*

Epictete was born from a vision to create a dining space that transcends mere sustenance. The founders sought to build a restaurant where:

- Every meal becomes a meditation
- Quality ingredients speak for themselves
- Design and atmosphere elevate the experience
- Guests leave not just satisfied, but enriched

### The Farm
The commitment to organic, farm-fresh ingredients isn't just marketing — it's philosophy in action. By cultivating their own produce, Epictete practices what Stoics called *oikeiosis* — the recognition of our connection to nature and community.

> "🌱 Bio de notre ferme" (Organic from our farm)

**The Olive Heritage:**
The farm's primary focus is **olive cultivation and olive oil production** — a tradition deeply rooted in Mediterranean culture. This olive oil project operates as a separate commercial venture, with premium olive oil available for retail sale beyond the restaurant.

This tagline represents:
- Direct connection to the land
- Control over ingredient quality
- Sustainability commitment
- Transparency in sourcing
- **Vertical integration** — from grove to table

### The Space
Every design choice at Epictete reflects intentionality:

- **Olive Trees** — Symbol of Mediterranean heritage and peace
- **Natural Light** — Connecting interior to exterior, day to nature
- **Warm Materials** — Wood, marble, brass create organic luxury
- **The É Monogram** — A meditative focal point, lit like a altar to good taste
- **Open Kitchen** — Transparency, nothing hidden

## The Laurel Wreath Symbol

### Historical Significance
The laurel wreath (couronne de laurier) has been a symbol of:
- **Victory** — Awarded to champions in ancient Greece
- **Achievement** — Worn by Roman emperors and poets
- **Excellence** — Associated with Apollo, god of arts
- **Immortality** — Evergreen laurel represented eternal honor

### At Epictete
The É enclosed in laurel wreaths represents:
- Excellence in cuisine
- Honor to the philosophical tradition
- Mediterranean heritage
- The victory of quality over mediocrity
